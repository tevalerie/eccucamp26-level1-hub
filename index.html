§ 01 · The Journey

Four weeks. Four verbs. One prototype.

Each week is one movement in a compounding sequence. Miss a waypoint and the next one costs more; the map isn't shy about it.

Week 01
Deconstruct
Take one bot apart. Meet variables, strings, functions, and the A.R.T. classifier by Day 5.
Week 02
Design
Prompt-vs-Context split, few-shot, and persona anchoring — the real prompt engineering week.
Week 03
Build
Retrieval, chain-of-thought as visible planning, guardrails, and agent loops that actually close.
Week 04
Pitch
Testing + QA only. Confirmation register, cross-pod crash-test, 5-beat pitch, Ship-or-Wait.

§ 09 · The Toolbox

Free tools first. Paid only where it counts.

The 2026 stack prioritises free tools at every teaching moment — pods deploy without per-camper costs, and teens learn responsible provider selection from day one. Two providers anchor it: Google AI Studio + Gemini (free) and OpenAI GPT (the paid, industry-standard teaching example).

Free-first is a curriculum position, not a budget accident. Every skeleton runs a fallback chain — free provider first, paid alternative behind it — so campers learn that choosing a model is an engineering decision with a cost column.

§ 10 · Safety & Judgement

The bot suggests. A human decides.

Care-first is a design principle, not a slogan. We name where it lives in the pipeline so pods know when to slow down.

What we mean when we say "care-first."

Any answer that touches money, health, family, immigration, or bereavement gets a slower path — a Persona Anchor pinned, PII redacted, a human name attached to the decision, and an escalation route that's actually used.

Autonomy ceiling

Bot suggests, human decides.

Named Day 1. Re-anchored Day 16. If the pod can't say who the human is, the artefact isn't ready.

PII hygiene

No real names in prompts.

Introduced Day 7 with the few-shot template. Redaction is a habit, not a rule they read once.

Guardrails wall

Six-check pre-response gate.

Built Day 14, used Day 18. The wall is a physical checklist on the studio wall, not a comment in code.

Ship-or-Wait

Signed register or wait.

Day 17 confirmation register. Client-facing claim ledger. If any Sev-1 is open, the pod waits — that's the whole rule.
