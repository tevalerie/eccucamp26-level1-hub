Parents

What your camper is building, how to help without doing it for them, and where the extra resources live — during camp and after it ends.

Weekly digest · sample — Week 1

This week: they took a bot apart.

Your camper probed two real chatbots built by past cohorts, mapped what works and what fails, and wrote their first Python — variables, inputs, and a small function of their own. By Friday their pod had a working classifier that knows when to hand a hard case to a human.

  • Ask them: "Where did the bot break when you tested it?" — they'll talk for ten minutes.
  • At home: the Free Course lessons 1–4 mirror this week, if they want a second pass.
  • Thursday: parent check-in evening — 15 minutes, on the island schedule in the Bulletin.

How to help

  • 01Let them drive. The camp's one rule applies at home too — the bot suggests, the human decides. Same for parents: guide, don't take the keyboard.
  • 02Charged laptop, labelled charger. The unglamorous half of readiness. The Bulletin says it weekly for a reason.
  • 03After camp ends, the hub stays open. The Free Course, the gallery, and the curriculum stay live all year — momentum doesn't have to stop in August.
  • 04Watch a past showcase together. The Past Camps row on Home shows exactly where four weeks of work lands.
